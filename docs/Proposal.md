Option B: CUHK Second-hand Marketplace SaaS 

Pain Points & Solutions 

1. The identity of the buyer and the seller is unknown, leading to safety concerns in using traditional second-hand marketplaces. Current second-hand marketplaces, such as Carousell, do not conduct any user screening, meaning anyone can create an account and start buying and selling. This has led to scams which result in real users losing their items or money. 

We plan to curb this issue by limiting who can use this platform. Namely, we will require users to input their university ID and their hostel if they have one, when they register an account. This ensures that all users are from CUHK and can be held accountable if they attempt to scam others. 

 

2. Existing second-hand marketplace platforms allows users from all over the city to buy items, including those that might be difficult to transport, for example, heavy or fragile items. Therefore, it might be difficult for university members to handle the logistics of delivering sold items. 

With our SaaS platform, we ensure that all listed marketplace items are sold within the university since only members of CUHK are allowed to buy and sell items, ensuring that users will never have to transport items to far-off locations, it also makes arranging meetup easier. 

 

Advanced Features 

1. Fuzzy search in the search function, allowing users to easily find items, even if they have made a typo or only have a vague description of the item. 

2. Real-time notifications for the seller when a buyer clicks the ‘Interested’ or ‘Buy’ button, diminishing time between communication and allows for quicker selling process. 

3. Utilizing chart.js to show different graphs, such as the price history of the same type of items.
